PETRA Ready Mixed Concrete – Important Commercial and Technical Terms At PETRA Ready Mixed Concrete, we are dedicated to delivering high-quality products and reliable services while maintaining clear, fair, and transparent terms of supply. The following points outline key conditions that apply to all our ready-mixed concrete deliveries in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia: Pricing and MeasurementAll ready-mixed concrete is sold and invoiced strictly on a per cubic meter (m³) basis. The volume is determined using calibrated equipment at our batching plants in accordance with industry standards and Saudi regulatory requirements. This ensures accurate measurement, consistency, and fairness in billing. No other unit of measurement (such as per load, per truck, or weight-based) will be used unless explicitly agreed upon in writing for special cases. Delivery Schedules and ConditionsWhile we make every effort to adhere to the agreed delivery schedule, delivery times are estimates onlyand remain subject to external factors beyond our direct control. These factors include, but are not limited to: Prevailing traffic conditions on public roads Site accessibility (e.g., road width, ground conditions, entry/exit restrictions, or pump setup requirements) Weather conditions (extreme heat, wind, rain, or sandstorms common in the region) Delays caused by client-side site readiness or coordination issues We will communicate any anticipated delays as soon as possible and work closely with you to find the best possible solution. Please ensure the pouring site is fully prepared and accessible upon the arrival of our mixer trucks to avoid additional waiting charges or rescheduling. Quality Claims and Acceptance CriteriaWe stand behind the quality of every batch of concrete supplied, produced under strict quality control procedures and in compliance with relevant Saudi Building Code (SBC) requirements, SASO standards, and ASTM/BS equivalents commonly referenced in the Kingdom. Any claims regarding concrete quality (particularly compressive strength) must be formally submitted in writing and supported by independent, accredited third-party laboratory test results. Tests must be conducted on properly taken, cured, and tested specimens (typically 150 mm cubes or cylinders). The standard acceptance criterion is based on the 28-day compressive strength results, unless otherwise specified in the project documents or contract. Claims must be submitted within 28 days of the actual pouring date to allow for timely investigation and resolution. Late or unsupported claims may not be entertained. We maintain full records of batching, testing, and delivery for traceability. These terms are designed to protect both parties, promote smooth project execution, and align with common industry practices in Saudi Arabia. We remain fully committed to partnering with you for successful project outcomes. For any clarifications, special requirements, or questions about these terms, please contact our sales or technical team. We value your trust and look forward to supporting your construction needs. Thank you for choosing PETRA Ready Mixed Concrete!
